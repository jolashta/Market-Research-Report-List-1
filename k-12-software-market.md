<p><p>The K-12 software market is categorized into two primary types: cloud-based and on-premises solutions. Cloud-based software is hosted online, offering schools flexibility, easy access, and reduced IT maintenance costs, making it ideal for collaborative learning environments. In contrast, on-premises software is installed locally on school servers, providing greater control over data security and customization, but requiring more significant upfront investments and ongoing maintenance. Both types cater to different institutional needs, budgets, and infrastructural capabilities.</p></p>
