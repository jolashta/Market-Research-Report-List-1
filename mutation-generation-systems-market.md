<p><p>Mutation generation systems encompass various market types based on the nature of genetic alterations. Missense mutations change a single amino acid in a protein, while nonsense mutations introduce premature stop codons. Insertions and deletions modify the DNA sequence by adding or removing nucleotides, potentially disrupting protein function. Duplications result in repeated segments of DNA, while frame shift mutations alter the reading frame, creating entirely different proteins. Repeat expansions involve the increase of specific nucleotide sequences, impacting gene function significantly.</p></p>
