<p><strong>Market Segmentation</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Waterborne Polyurethane Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Anionic PUDs</li><li>Cationic PUDs</li><li>Non-Ionic PUDs</li><li>Component Polyurethane</li><li>Two-component Polyurethane</li><li>Urethane-modified</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The waterborne polyurethane market encompasses various types tailored for specific applications. Anionic PUDs utilize negatively charged emulsifiers, enhancing stability in coatings. Cationic PUDs, with positively charged components, excel in adhesion and antimicrobial properties. Non-Ionic PUDs offer versatility across different pH levels without affecting properties. Component polyurethanes consist of separate prepolymer and curing agents, allowing customizable formulations. Two-component polyurethanes combine these components at the time of use for enhanced performance. Urethane-modified options incorporate urethane groups into other polymers for improved characteristics.</p></p>
