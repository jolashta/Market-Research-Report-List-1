<p><p>Acoustic wave sensors in biology utilize sound waves to detect biological entities with high sensitivity and specificity. The Quartz Crystal Microbalance (QCM) sensor measures mass changes on its surface by monitoring frequency shifts, making it ideal for detecting biomolecular interactions. Surface Acoustic Wave (SAW) sensors, on the other hand, use surface waves to sense changes in mass or chemical composition, allowing versatile applications in pathogen detection and environmental monitoring. Both types enhance biosensing capabilities in research and medical diagnostics.</p></p>
