<p><p>The Surgical Aortic Valve Replacement market encompasses various approaches to address aortic stenosis. The transfemoral approach involves accessing the heart through the femoral artery, offering minimal invasiveness and faster recovery. The transapical approach, involving direct access through the apex of the heart, is suitable for patients with peripheral vascular disease. Other approaches may include the subclavian or direct aortic route. Each method has its indications, benefits, and risks, catering to diverse patient needs based on their medical conditions and anatomical considerations.</p></p>
